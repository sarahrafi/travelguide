Sites - Toulouse

Toulouse Museum

Not a museum about Toulouse, but the largest institution of ethnological and natural history in France outside Paris: the Toulouse Museum displays more than 2.5 million exhibits.If you have something to do with the natural sciences, you can indulge in botanical, insect, mineralogical, ornithology, paleontology and many other "logical" exhibitions for hours!

Bemberg Foundation

Hotel Hôtel d'Assézat, the privileged home of the Bemberg Foundation, is a marvel in itself.Designed by Nicolas Bachelet, Toulouse's oldest architect in the Renaissance, this palace took almost 30 years to complete.It was built for Pierre Dacisat, who built his fortune on the Wood trade, but was bankrupt by the time his opulent palace was completed in 1581.

Place du Capitole

The hub of Toulouse life is the bustling Place du Capitole. This town square showcases the classic architecture of Toulouse. On the west side of the square, there are beautiful arcades. On the east side of the square is the celebrated 18th-century Capitole building.
